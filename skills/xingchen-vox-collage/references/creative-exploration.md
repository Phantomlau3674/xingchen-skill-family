# Creative Exploration And Visual Ambition

Working notes for the `Explore Before You Commit` step in [SKILL.md](../SKILL.md). The exploration is free-form prose, an authoring default, and never validator input.

## Visual Ambition Paragraph For Bounded Briefs

When a root agent delegates a bounded implementation task, paste this paragraph into the brief and adapt it to the scene. It is provider-neutral and deliberately carries no numeric quotas:

> Aim high visually. Source or generate independent materials for this scene instead of flattening everything into one plate. Give the frame one clear composition with an unmistakable focal carrier and intentional negative space. Let motion explain something — an entrance that establishes hierarchy, a transformation that shows cause, a settle that marks resolution — rather than adding movement for energy. If any passage turns sparse or repetitive, rescue it by changing the spatial mechanism, scale, material, or crop, not by stacking more layers or effects. Exact text, numbers, and evidence stay code-native and readable at phone size.

## Example Exploration (Fictional, Publication-Safe)

Topic: why the writing on a thermal receipt fades away. All characters and objects below are original paper-collage inventions.

> Two routes could carry this film: a chemistry route that turns the receipt into a lab bench of paper test strips, or a time-lapse route that treats the receipt as a tiny landscape slowly bleached by sun and time. I choose the lab bench, because the fading is a chemical story and the bench lets heat, light, and air each appear as a physical actor. The material opportunities are unusually rich: thermal paper itself is the substrate, so the film can live on one long roll of receipt paper; a paper-cut lab technician moth, "Doctor Dot," can guide the eye without ever carrying text; heat can be a vermilion iron-shaped cutout that stamps letters into existence and later steals them back. The sparse risk is the middle — once the mechanism is explained, two more examples of fading would repeat the same picture — so the middle passage changes mechanism instead: the receipt is folded into a timeline accordion, and each fold is a month the ink survives. Motion carries the meaning: the iron stamps, the moth peels a letter off the page to show dye molecules letting go, and the accordion settles flat when the receipt is finally blank. Nothing moves just to sparkle.

## Case-Study Note: Observed Density Facts, Never Quotas

One successful 86-second production in this visual language shipped with 8 scenes, 103 layers, and 78 approved assets. These are observed facts from that single production, recorded here only to show what one healthy film happened to contain. They are not targets, recommended ranges, or quotas of any kind. A stronger film may use fewer or more; the ambition paragraph above deliberately prescribes no counts, and no review should ever cite these numbers as a bar to clear.
