# Editorial Collage Visual Language

Use this reference to write `visual/vox/DESIGN.md` and judge hero frames.

## The Visual Thesis

Define one sentence describing how the subject becomes a material world. Avoid naming a style alone.

Good:

> The argument appears as evidence clipped onto a warm newsroom worktable; one vermilion mark interrupts monochrome source fragments as the causal chain is assembled.

Weak:

> Vox style, paper collage, cool animation.

## Locked Style DNA

Lock these dimensions before expanding scenes:

1. `substrate`: off-white paper, kraft worktable, flat color field, archive sheet, or another film-specific ground;
2. `edge_language`: torn, scissor-cut, or geometric; choose a dominant family;
3. `image_treatment`: full color, monochrome, duotone, halftone, engraving, or screenprint;
4. `palette`: substrate, paper, ink, one primary accent, and at most one supporting accent;
5. `shadow_logic`: one direction, one depth range, one softness family;
6. `grain`: paper fiber, risograph, newsprint, photocopy, or clean editorial;
7. `type_roles`: display, information, data, and subtitle roles using project-approved fonts;
8. `annotation`: marker, pencil, grease pencil, stamp, tape, or editorial rule;
9. `motion_character`: precise, punchy, restrained, elastic, urgent, or lyrical;
10. `anti_reference`: what must not appear.

## Composition

- Give the frame one unmistakable argument carrier.
- Use scale contrast before decoration.
- Place generated cutouts directly on the substrate or a localized color field. Do not wrap every asset in another paper card.
- Use foreground occluders to create shallow depth and reveal direction.
- Keep evidence larger and quieter than decoration.
- Reserve negative space intentionally for live type, subtitles, or a later reveal.
- Let adjacent scenes vary scale, density, temperature, crop, or spatial mechanism for a semantic reason.

## Typography

Keep exact text live in Remotion. Use generated text only as deliberately unreadable texture.

- Display copy: short assertion, normally one or two lines.
- Information copy: labels, captions, source notes, and explanatory text.
- Data copy: years, percentages, prices, counters, and chart values.
- Subtitles: separate layer; never let a title composition determine caption placement.

For a 1920x1080 Douyin master, begin around 58-64 px subtitles, 44-52 px thesis labels, and 38-46 px important nodes. Test on a phone downsample.

## Vox Grammar Versus Collage Decoration

The branch is successful when the frame demonstrates an argument through one of these mechanisms:

- evidence is exposed and annotated;
- two states are compared in the same spatial system;
- a causal chain is assembled;
- a number changes scale against a stable reference;
- a map or timeline is traversed;
- an object transforms into the consequence it represents;
- an established pattern is interrupted and reframed.

Paper texture without one of these jobs is decoration.

## Hero Frame Checklist

- Can a cold viewer name the focal object in one second?
- Can they infer the relationship without production notes?
- Does the visual remain specific with subtitles hidden?
- Are source proof and generated illustration visually distinguishable?
- Does every independently moving actor exist as a layer or code-native object?
- Is the final composition worth holding for at least half a second?
