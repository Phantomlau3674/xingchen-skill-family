# Scene Forces

Every scene should be directed from forces, not templates.

## Core Questions

Before choosing any layout or motion, answer:

- what must the viewer believe after this scene
- what must the viewer feel during this scene
- what must the viewer remember one minute later
- what is the scene job: `prove`, `announce`, `contrast`, `explain`, or `release`

## Force Axes

Score each axis as `low`, `medium`, or `high`:

- `trust_need`: how much the scene must earn belief
- `spectacle_need`: how much the scene must hit emotionally
- `density`: how much information must be processed quickly
- `pace`: how hard the scene should move
- `proof_dependence`: whether literal pixels matter

## Job Tendencies

- `prove`: trust beats spectacle
- `announce`: speed and clarity beat structure
- `contrast`: tension and winner-loser hierarchy matter
- `explain`: structure and sequencing matter
- `release`: emotional payoff or compression matters

## Output

These force calls should directly shape:

- dominant anchor
- camera intent
- subtitle avoidance
- proof visibility
- motion verbs
