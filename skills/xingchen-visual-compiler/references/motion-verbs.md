# Motion Verbs

Use motion verbs as reusable movement language. Combine only the verbs the scene earns.

## Allowed Verbs

- `type`: characters or interface elements appear operationally
- `lock-on`: camera or highlight settles onto the key area
- `scan`: a focus band or box walks the proof region
- `crack`: a split, tear, or rupture reveals conflict
- `pulse`: controlled emphasis on a keyword or object
- `drift`: low-amplitude ambient movement that keeps a scene alive
- `stack`: layered items accumulate into a build-up
- `collapse`: multiple items compress into one conclusion
- `reveal`: hidden content becomes legible or dominant
- `whip`: hard directional boundary motion between scenes

## Rules

- every verb must support the dominant anchor
- proof scenes should prefer `lock-on`, `scan`, or restrained `reveal`
- contrast scenes may use `crack` or `collapse`
- do not stack verbs just because they exist
- if a scene cannot name its motion verb, the scene direction is not ready
